# IBMA-DoG-turbulence-mitigation
These video clips demonstrate the atmospheric turbulence mitigation performance of our proposed algorithm in the short-wave infrared (SWIR) band, covering both static scenes and dynamic scenarios with moving targets.
Registration Demonstration (Corresponding to Fig. 8): This section presents two sets of registration results. 'RAW' denotes the original turbulent sequence. Block-Optical-Hybrid display the alignment of the original sequence using the following methods: Block Matching, Optical Flow, and Hybrid Registration.
Performance Analysis on Static Scenes (Corresponding to Figs. 14 & 15): This section is divided into two groups to compare performance with and without the integration of the DoG&DDE module. 'RAW' represents the original turbulent video, while 'RAW-MAP' denotes a simple linear mapping applied to the raw data to highlight turbulence effects. The remaining entries are turbulence mitigation algorithms. 'IBMA' and 'IBMA-DoG' are our proposed methods; the sole difference lies in the integration of the DoG&DDE module. Note that 'DoG&DDE' refers to image enhancement based on the Difference of Gaussian filter. Since some comparative algorithms yield only static images, this video exclusively showcases results from algorithms capable of processing video streams.
Performance Analysis on Scenes with Moving Targets (Corresponding to Fig. 16): This section demonstrates the algorithm's performance in three distinct scenarios involving moving targets. The footage was captured using two different SWIR imaging devices and sourced from a public dataset. Similar to the above, 'IBMA' and 'IBMA-DoG' represent our proposed methods, distinguished by the presence or absence of the DoG&DDE module.
The results demonstrate that our method achieves significant atmospheric turbulence mitigation in SWIR videos, effectively reducing image jitter and geometric distortion while enhancing detail clarity.
